# Responsive Search Ads and Landing Pages

The ad and the landing page are one unit. A great ad pointed at a generic homepage wastes the click. Match the promise end to end.

## Responsive Search Ads (RSAs)
RSAs mix up to 15 headlines and 4 descriptions into combinations Google serves dynamically.

- **Headlines**: write 8-12 genuinely different ones, not 12 rewordings. Cover: the core value prop, the specific outcome, social proof, a differentiator, the offer/CTA, and the keyword theme. Follow [writing-style.md](../../../ads-foundations/writing-style.md) - no "unlock", no "seamless".
- **Descriptions**: 3-4. Lead with the outcome, back it with proof, end with a clear CTA.
- **Pinning**: pin sparingly. Pin only what *must* always show (a compliance line, your brand in position 1 for brand campaigns). Over-pinning kills Google's ability to optimize and tanks Ad Strength. Pin position 1 for one critical headline, leave the rest free.
- **Ad Strength** is a guide, not a grade that wins auctions - but "Poor" usually means you gave it too few or too-similar assets. Aim for "Good"+ without pinning everything.
- Run **2-3 RSAs per ad group** and read asset-level performance. Replace "Low" assets, keep "Best".

## Write to the query intent
- Brand ad: reassurance + direct CTA ("Official site. Start free.").
- High-intent non-brand: the specific outcome and differentiator ("Book 3x more meetings. No data setup.").
- Competitor: the honest contrast, no trashing ("Switching from [X]? Faster setup, no per-seat fees.").
Put the keyword theme in at least one headline - relevance lifts CTR and Quality Score.

## Landing pages
The fastest CPL win is usually the page, not the bid.
- **Message match**: the page headline echoes the ad promise and the query. Click "ABM platform for agencies" -> land on a page that says exactly that, not the generic homepage.
- **One job per page**: one offer, one primary CTA. Kill the nav-away links and the five competing CTAs.
- **Speed**: slow pages lose mobile clicks and drag Quality Score. Test it.
- **Proof above the fold**: logos, a number, a quote. B2B buyers need trust fast.
- **Form length = intent gate**: short forms = more leads, lower quality. Longer/qualified forms = fewer, better. Match form length to the funnel stage and what you'll feed back as a conversion.

## Quality Score, practically
QS (1-10, keyword level) is built from expected CTR, ad relevance, and landing page experience. Low QS = higher CPC for the same position. When a keyword has low QS, fix the weak component (usually ad relevance or the landing page) before throwing bid at it.
