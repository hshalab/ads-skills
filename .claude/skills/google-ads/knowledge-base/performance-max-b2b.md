# Performance Max for B2B

PMax runs across all of Google's inventory (Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Maps, Discover) with one campaign and heavy automation. It's powerful and it's a black box. For B2B, it's an amplifier you add *after* Search works - never the foundation.

## When to use it
- **After** high-intent Search proves unit economics and you have a real conversion signal (ideally offline-imported).
- When you have decent creative assets (images, logos, and at least some video - Google will auto-make bad video if you don't give it good).
- To scale beyond what pure Search volume allows, once you can afford the learning cost.

## When NOT to use it
- As your first campaign. You'll have no idea what's working and PMax won't tell you cleanly.
- With weak conversion tracking. PMax optimizes hard to whatever you call a conversion - feed it form fills and it finds you infinite junk.
- Tiny budgets. PMax needs room and time to learn; underfunded, it spends erratically.

## Guardrails (this is where B2B accounts get burned)
- **Account-level brand exclusions** - stop PMax from cannibalizing your brand Search and claiming credit for traffic you'd win for free.
- **Audience signals** - feed your best 1st-party data (customer lists, high-intent site visitors, CRM segments). Signals guide PMax; they don't hard-restrict it, but they matter a lot in B2B.
- **Negative keywords** - available at account level (and now campaign level for many accounts). Apply your junk list. Don't run PMax negative-free.
- **Conversion quality** - import offline conversions before scaling PMax, or it optimizes to lead volume, not pipeline.
- **Search themes** - use them to steer, but watch the search terms / insights to confirm it's not drifting into junk.

## Reading PMax
Visibility is limited by design. Lean on the Insights tab, asset group performance, and (crucially) your **CRM lead quality** by campaign. If PMax leads convert to pipeline at half the rate of Search leads, it's buying you cheap-looking, expensive-in-reality volume. Cut or re-signal.

## Honest take
For most B2B SaaS, the order of value is: Brand Search > high-intent non-brand Search > remarketing > PMax > broad demand-gen. PMax earns its slot only once the cheaper, clearer wins are maxed out.
